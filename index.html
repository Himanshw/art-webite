About Me - Shani Singh

Shani Singh: The Color of Revolution on the Canvas of the Inner Self
Rooted in the fertile soil of Ramnagar Powai, Azamgarh, Uttar Pradesh, Shani Singh is an extraordinary visionary from an ordinary farming family. His story is not merely a presentation of art, but a proclamation of the ignited spark that resides in every young heart. In the fourth grade, when a friend challenged his creative skills, he did not accept defeat. He transformed that challenge into his inherent strength and initiated a new revolution on the canvas of art.

Five years of relentless effort and burning passion transformed Shani not just into an artist, but into a rebellious creator. He even sidelined his academic education, as the tide of art surged within him. His art matured him so much that his Sanskrit teacher organized a competition between them, in which Shani emerged victorious. He converted his anger into positive energy and revived the suppressed artistic talent within him.

Shani Singh's art, a priceless treasure of our cultural heritage, is a proclamation of the revival of folk arts. He has a special interest in Mandala art and Patchwork paintings. His ultimate goal is to inspire young people and ignite within them the flame to create something extraordinary. He uses various mediums, including oil paints, charcoal, pencil, and animation, but the true medium of his art is his rebellious heart, which presents a new saga in every color.

Shani Singh has participated in significant exhibitions like "Echoes of Serenity" and "Divine Emergence" at Gandhi Darshan, Rajghat. He organized an art competition for child artists on the occasion of the Ram Mandir inauguration on January 22nd. His ultimate aim is to guide young people in the right direction and help them recognize their unique talents. He believes that art is not just a hobby, but an integral part of life, which connects us to our roots and empowers us to establish our identity.

Echo of Peace: The Musical Manifestation of the Soul

Shani Singh's "Echo of Peace" is not just a painting, but a musical manifestation of his inner self, the vocal expression of his soul. It is an expression of the period when he felt enveloped by worldly noise, pretense, and indifference. Life seemed like a pointless race, where one had to run without any destination. But within him, in the depths of his eyes, another world existedâ€”his countless dreams, twinkling like stars.

The vast eye depicted in "Echo of Peace" is not just a medium to see the world, but a window to his soul. It holds a strange pain, an anger perhaps towards himself, a despair that was emptying him from within, and a restlessness that wouldn't let him have a moment of peace. He was merely searching for a quiet place away from this noise, where he could hear the voice of his soul, where his dreams could turn into reality.

In that painting, within his eyes, hummingbirds appeared. A small bird sits within his eye, as if his suppressed desires and unexpressed thoughts are imprisoned in a secret dungeon. It symbolizes all the things he wanted to express, what he wanted to do, but couldn't due to some unknown fear. It was a silent cry of his conscience, suppressed but not dead.

And the other bird... it is taking a flight of freedom from that vast eye, just as depicted in the painting. It was his own restless desireâ€”a search for a new path, hope for a new beginning, the start of a new journey. The wings of that bird are adorned with the colors of the rainbowâ€”each color a new possibility, a ray of new hope.

Shani Singh was afraid, it's true. He hesitated to pursue his dreams. He feared he would never achieve them, that he would get lost in the noise of this world. But one day, a change occurred within him. He made a bold decisionâ€”to break his limitations, to leave his fear behind, and to enter the world of his dreams. Just as that hummingbird fearlessly flew from his eye in the painting, so did he spread the wings of his imagination and took a long flight. He set out in search of the peace that had always existed within him, only needing to be recognized and experienced.

"Echo of Peace" is not just a painting, but a saga of his soul, a living testament to his inner journey. It reminds him that hardships will come in life, but one should never abandon their dreams. It inspires him to give flight to his imagination and find the peace of his soul. Like that hummingbird, which flies freely in the painting, he also broke his limitations and breathed in the air of freedom. And those stars... they are the witnesses to the infinity of his dreams, countless possibilities. They assure him that nothing is impossible, only unwavering faith and determination are needed.

Currently, Shani Singh is working to revive the disappearing folk arts. His artistic aspiration is to unite all artists and help them progress through their art. He plans to organize exhibitions from time to time, so that young people can understand our cultural heritage and draw inspiration from it.

For Shani Singh, art is a new perspective on life, which empowers young people to change their mindset. He sees the world through his art as a place where young people are heading in the wrong direction without knowledge. He wants to show the right direction of art and ensure that art is not a substitute for artificial intelligence.
